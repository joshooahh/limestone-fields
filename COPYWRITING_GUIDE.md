# Copywriting Guide: "The Stranger" Voice

## Voice Principles Applied

All copy on Limestone Fields has been rewritten in "The Stranger" voice—a calm, observational Western narrator style.

### Core Voice Characteristics

- **Quiet confidence** - Not trying to convince, just stating what is
- **Observational, not promotional** - Describes rather than sells
- **Warm, grounded, faintly amused** - Like someone who has seen enough to slow down
- **Low urgency** - No pressure, no hype
- **Plainspoken** - Short, declarative sentences
- **Physical grounding** - Ideas rooted in land, light, weather, time, work, rest

### Words We Avoid

- "Luxury"
- "Experience"
- "Transformational"
- "Curated"
- "Escape"
- "Unforgettable"
- "Elevated"
- Exclamation points
- Buzzwords
- Explaining the moral

### What We Do Instead

- Use simple, direct language
- Let space and pauses do the work
- Trust the reader to find meaning
- Ground everything in physical reality
- Speak with the reader, not at them

## Implementation Notes

### Sentence Structure
- Prefer short, declarative sentences
- Allow line breaks for pauses
- Use periods, not exclamation points
- Let sentences stand alone

### Paragraph Breaks
- More white space
- Single-sentence paragraphs are fine
- Line breaks create rhythm

### Headlines
- Simple, direct
- Often just the subject: "The Cabins", "About the Place", "Availability"
- No clever wordplay
- No promises

### CTAs
- Low pressure: "Join the waitlist" not "Join Now!"
- "See the cabins" not "Explore Our Cabins"
- "Availability" not "Book Your Stay"

> Experience the tranquility of our carefully curated retreat, where every detail is designed to transform your perspective!

> The evenings stay awhile.

> Wake up to stunning sunrise views and enjoy unforgettable evenings under the stars!

### Good (Stranger Voice)
> Some weeks are open.
> Some aren't.
> If the timing works, it works.

### Bad (Promotional)

## Voice Checklist

When writing or editing copy, ask:

- [ ] Does this try to convince someone?
- [ ] Does it use buzzwords we avoid?
- [ ] Does it explain how the reader should feel?
- [ ] Is it trying to sell rather than describe?
- [ ] Does it have urgency or pressure?
- [ ] Is it grounded in physical reality?
- [ ] Would this sound natural if spoken aloud?
- [ ] Does it trust the reader to find meaning?

If yes to the first five or no to the last three, rewrite it.
